week 2

Kinetic Sculpture

For my kinetic sculpture I worked with Sofia and Emma to create a merry go-round with fish organised into the food chain. A cell, then a shrimp, a small fish, a big fish, and a shark. First, the fish were sketched into an online illustration app and were uploaded to fusion. A cylinder with an aquatic wave pattern on the rim, a circular base and smaller rotating disk to attach the fish on to. Then the fish were duplicated so that each of them could be double sides. They were laser cut from cardboard and hot glued onto tooth picks. Then the toothpicks were attached to a cardboard disk We used a motor to make the fish spin. We attached the disk to the motor nub with a cardboard support. We attached the motor onto the base circle and used cardboard to support and stabilize it.We used a USB port as a power source which connected to an outlet. We had two problems. The first was that the way the motor was spinning made the fish swim backwards. We fixed this by flipping the motor upside down and later on realized there are much easier methods of changing the direction of the motor. We did end up solving the issue though. The second problem was that the fish swam very fast. When the sculpture was powered up none of our beautiful details were visible because they were spinning too fast. We tried to solve this problem electronically by adding resisters, but all of the resisters were too strong. We concluded that altering the circuitry wouldn’t solve the speed issue, so we gave up. It still spins very fast when plugged in.
